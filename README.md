
# Mac Running Slow After Update? Why Is My Mac So Slow? Best Ways to Fix a Slow Mac

## Introduction

If your Mac suddenly feels sluggish after installing a system update, you're probably wondering: **"why is my Mac running slow after update"** or **"how to fix slow Mac"**.

This is a very common issue. After updates, macOS performs background tasks that can temporarily or permanently affect performance.

This guide will show you exactly **why your Mac is slow** and how to fix it step by step.

---

## Why Is My Mac So Slow After an Update?

Here are the most common causes:

### 1. Spotlight Reindexing

After updates, macOS rebuilds search indexes, which can heavily use CPU.

### 2. Background System Optimization

macOS may:

* Reorganize files
* Optimize storage
* Sync iCloud data

### 3. App Compatibility Issues

Older apps may not be optimized for the new macOS version.

### 4. Increased System Requirements

New updates often require more RAM and CPU power.

### 5. Corrupted Cache or System Files

Updates can sometimes leave behind broken cache files.

### 3. Reindex Spotlight (If Stuck)

Reindexing Spotlight can fix this issue and significantly improve performance.

Step 1: Open Spotlight Settings
Click the Apple menu () in the top-left corner
Go to System Settings
Scroll down and select Siri & Spotlight
Step 2: Open Spotlight Privacy
Scroll to find Spotlight Privacy
Click it

What Happens Next

### 8. Reset NVRAM / SMC

Reset NVRAM (Intel Macs Only)

NVRAM stores settings like:

Startup disk
Display resolution
System preferences
Steps:
Shut down your Mac
Turn it on and immediately press:
Option (⌥) + Command (⌘) + P + R
Hold the keys for about 20 seconds
Release and let your Mac boot normally
Reset SMC (Intel Macs Only)

SMC controls:

Power management
Battery behavior
Fans and thermal system
For MacBooks (with T2 chip or Intel):
Shut down your Mac
Press and hold:
Shift + Control + Option (left side) + Power button
Hold for 10 seconds
Release all keys
Turn your Mac back on
For Apple Silicon Macs (M1, M2, M3)

👉 Good news:
You don’t need to reset NVRAM or SMC manually

Just:
Shut down your Mac
Wait 10–15 seconds
Turn it back on

macOS handles this automatically.

Why This Fix Works

Resetting NVRAM/SMC can fix:

Mac running slow
Power-related performance issues
Overheating or fan problems
Random system glitches

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How can I fix a slow boot on Mac?

Slow startup usually happens because of too many login items, low available storage, or outdated system components. To fix this, remove unnecessary startup apps, clear disk space, and make sure your macOS is up to date. Macs with SSDs also boot much faster than those with traditional hard drives.

---

### Why is my Mac running slow after an update?

After installing an update, macOS often performs background tasks like indexing and optimization. This can temporarily slow things down. If the issue continues, check for outdated or incompatible apps, free up storage, and install any additional updates.

---

### Does low storage affect Mac performance?

Yes. When your storage is almost full, macOS has less space to manage system processes and memory efficiently. This can lead to noticeable slowdowns and lag.

---

### How much free space should I keep on my Mac?

It’s recommended to keep at least **10–15% of your disk space free**. This helps macOS run smoothly, handle updates, and manage temporary files without performance issues.

---

### Why is my Mac slow even if I don’t have many apps?

Even with few visible apps, your Mac may still run background processes, browser tasks, or outdated software. In some cases, malware or hidden processes can also impact performance.

---

### Can malware slow down a Mac?

Yes. Malicious or unwanted programs can use system resources like CPU, RAM, and internet bandwidth, which can make your Mac feel slow and unresponsive.

---

### How can I find what is slowing down my Mac?

Open **Activity Monitor** to check which processes are using the most CPU or memory. You should also review storage usage and startup items to identify potential issues.

---

### Does closing apps improve performance?

Yes. Closing apps you’re not using helps free up system resources, especially on Macs with limited RAM or when multitasking heavily.

---

### Will upgrading to an SSD make my Mac faster?

Absolutely. Switching from a traditional hard drive to an SSD can dramatically improve boot speed, file access, and overall system performance.

---

### Does resetting NVRAM or SMC help with performance?

On Intel-based Macs, resetting NVRAM or SMC can fix certain system or power-related issues. On Apple Silicon Macs, these processes are handled automatically when you restart the device.
